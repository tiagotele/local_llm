Peter Lynch is an American investor, mutual fund manager, and philanthropist, widely recognized for his work managing the Magellan Fund at Fidelity Investments from 1977 to 1990. During his tenure, Lynch achieved an average annual return of 29.2%, significantly outperforming the broader market and making Magellan one of the best-performing mutual funds of its time. Lynch’s investment philosophy emphasized “buying what you know,” advocating for investments in companies whose business models and products investors can easily understand.

After retiring in 1990, Lynch dedicated himself to philanthropy and authored several books on investing, including the popular “One Up on Wall Street” and “Beating the Street.” His books and investment strategies continue to influence investors around the world, cementing his status as one of the greatest mutual fund managers in history.
